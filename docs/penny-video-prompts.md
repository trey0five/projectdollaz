# Homepage Hero Video — Gemini / Veo Prompts

**Story:** A school business manager drowning in a trial balance → Penny arrives → relief.

**Brand:** Navy `#1f3d72` (the platform) + Penny gold (the mascot/AI). The whole film is built on
one visual metaphor: **cold blue-grey chaos → warm gold clarity.**

---

## ⚠️ Read this before generating

Veo cannot reliably render (a) your specific Penny coin mascot or (b) legible spreadsheet/UI text
on a monitor. Asking for either burns credits and returns a garbled blob + gibberish cells.

**Hybrid approach (recommended):**

| Layer | Source |
|---|---|
| Human, room, emotion, light shift | **Veo** (prompts below) |
| Penny coin + real app UI | **Composited in post** (your real SVG + a screen recording) |

Every prompt below keeps the monitor **out of focus or hidden behind screen glare**, and stages
Penny's arrival as **warm gold light blooming from the screen**. That gives you a clean plate to
composite onto — and makes the text-garbling problem impossible by construction.

**Also:** Veo clips are ~8 seconds. A struggle→relief arc will not fit in one clip. Generate the
4 shots below separately and cut them together (~30s), or use the single-shot version if you only
want one looping hero moment.

## SHOT 1 — The Struggle (8s)

```
Cinematic medium close-up, shallow depth of field. A tired woman in her late 30s — a
private-school business manager — sits alone at a cluttered desk in a small school office
late at night. She is surrounded by stacks of printed spreadsheets and manila folders. She
leans toward a computer monitor, squinting, one hand pressed to her temple, the other
scrolling a mouse wheel over and over. She exhales slowly, defeated.

The monitor is turned away from camera at an angle so the screen content is not legible —
only a cold blue-white glow spills across her face and the papers. Harsh overhead
fluorescent light, cold blue-grey color grade, muted desaturated tones. A cold cup of coffee
sits beside the keyboard.

Camera: slow, almost imperceptible push-in on her face. Handheld, subtle.
Mood: exhausted, overwhelmed, isolated.
Audio: soft hum of fluorescent lights, the clicking of a mouse wheel, a quiet tired sigh,
distant HVAC. No music.
Style: photorealistic corporate documentary, cinematic 35mm, natural film grain.
```

## SHOT 2 — The Wall (8s)

```
Cinematic close-up, shallow depth of field. The same woman removes her glasses and presses
the heels of both palms into her eyes. She drops her hands and stares blankly off-screen,
utterly stuck. Beside her, a thick stack of printed ledger pages is covered in red pen marks
and sticky notes. She picks up one page, looks at it, and lets it fall back onto the pile.

Cold blue-grey fluorescent light, desaturated. The computer monitor behind her is visible only
as a soft out-of-focus blue bokeh glow — no readable text.

Camera: static locked-off shot, slight handheld drift.
Mood: hitting a wall, quiet frustration.
Audio: paper rustling, a pen being set down, a long exhale, fluorescent hum.
Style: photorealistic, cinematic 35mm, natural film grain.
```

## SHOT 3 — Penny Arrives (8s) ⭐ the money shot

```
Cinematic medium shot, shallow depth of field. The same woman at the same desk. A soft warm
GOLDEN light begins to bloom and pulse from the computer monitor, gradually washing out the
cold blue fluorescent tone of the room. The golden glow spreads across her face, catches in
her glasses, and warms the stacks of paper around her.

She slowly lifts her head. Her eyes widen. Her expression shifts from exhaustion to curiosity
to dawning relief. She sits up straighter, leaning IN toward the screen instead of slumping
away from it. A small disbelieving smile begins at the corner of her mouth.

The monitor is angled away from camera — the screen content is NOT visible or legible, only
its warm golden light. The room's color grade transitions from cold blue-grey to warm gold
over the course of the shot.

Camera: slow push-in toward her face as the light warms.
Mood: hope arriving, a weight lifting, quiet wonder.
Audio: the fluorescent hum fades away; a soft warm ascending chime; a small surprised intake
of breath. Gentle, hopeful.
Style: photorealistic, cinematic 35mm, warm golden hour lighting, natural film grain.
```

> **Post note:** This is the shot you composite the real Penny coin onto — have her spin up out
> of the golden bloom with her gold halo. The light is already motivated in-camera, so she'll sit
> in the plate naturally.

The room is now bathed in warm golden light. Warm color grade, soft and inviting. The monitor

Camera: slow gentle pull-back, revealing the calm, tidy desk.
Mood: relief, confidence, control regained, quiet triumph.
Audio: a soft relieved laugh, papers being set aside, warm ambient tone. Uplifting, calm.
Style: photorealistic, cinematic 35mm, warm cinematic lighting, natural film grain.

## Production settings

| Setting | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Aspect ratio | **16:9** | Homepage hero |
| Resolution | **1080p** | Upscale in post if needed |
| Audio | Generate it, but **ship the hero muted** | Browsers block autoplay audio; keep a sound-on version for social |
| Length | 4 × 8s → cut to **~24–30s** | Or loop Shot 3 + 4 for a short hero loop |
| Casting | Keep the **same wardrobe + desk description** across all 4 prompts | Veo has no character memory between clips — repeat her description verbatim to keep her consistent |

## Post-production checklist

1. Cut Shots 1→4. Land the cut to Shot 3 on the first frame of the gold bloom.
2. **Composite the real Penny SVG** rising out of the golden bloom in Shot 3, with her gold halo + a blink.
3. **Composite a real screen recording** of the app (trial balance → generated statements) onto the monitor, or show it in a floating navy UI card next to her. Corner-pin track it.
4. Grade: push Shots 1–2 colder (blue-grey), Shots 3–4 warmer (Penny gold).
5. End card: navy background, Penny coin, tagline, CTA.

## Consistency tip

If your Veo version supports an **image-to-video / reference frame**, generate one still of the
woman at the desk first, then feed that same still into all four shots. That's the single most
reliable way to keep her face and the room identical across clips.

# CONCEPT 2 — "Room of experts → one person" (consolidation)

**Story:** A whole floor of financial analysts grinding in cubicles → it all consolidates
down to ONE calm person at a computer with the FinRep dashboard up.

**Metaphor:** crowded / cold / many → focused / warm-gold / one. The work of a department,
done by one person + Penny.

> Same rule as Concept 1: keep the dashboard OUT of focus (navy + gold glow only) and composite
> the real UI in post. Veo will garble real charts and text.

## SHOT 1 — The Army (8s)

```
Cinematic wide establishing shot, slow motion. A large open-plan corporate office floor packed
with rows of identical cubicles, seen from a high angle. Dozens of financial analysts in
business attire hunch over desks buried in printed spreadsheets, ledgers, and glowing monitors.
Phones ring, people shuffle stacks of paper, a printer spits out pages. Everyone looks busy,
overworked, and slightly frantic. Cold blue-grey fluorescent lighting, desaturated corporate
color grade, faint haze in the air.

Camera: slow sweeping crane move drifting forward over the cubicle rows.
Mood: overwhelming scale, relentless manual labor, industrial busywork.
Audio: layered office din — ringing phones, keyboards, printers, overlapping chatter, paper.
Style: photorealistic corporate cinematography, cinematic 35mm, natural film grain.
```

## SHOT 2 — The Consolidation (8s) ⭐ signature shot

```
Cinematic shot, continuous motion. The same crowded office floor of analysts in cubicles. One
by one, the surrounding cubicles and workers gently fade and dissolve into soft particles of
warm golden light that drift inward toward the center of frame. The cold blue fluorescent tone
recedes as the golden light gathers. The rows of desks empty and dissolve in a smooth wave,
collapsing inward.

As everything else fades away, the camera pushes toward a single desk remaining in the center,
now lit with warm golden light — one calm person seated at a clean, uncluttered computer.

Camera: smooth push-in that follows the collapse toward the single central desk.
Mood: transformation, simplification, the many becoming one, relief and clarity.
Audio: the chaotic office din smoothly fades and filters down into a single soft warm ascending
chime and quiet calm ambience.
Style: photorealistic with tasteful particle-dissolve VFX, cinematic 35mm, warm golden grade.
```

## SHOT 3 — The One (8s)

```
Cinematic medium shot, shallow depth of field. A single calm, confident professional sits at a
clean modern desk in a now-quiet, warmly lit office, alone but relaxed and in control. They lean
comfortably toward a large monitor and interact with it easily — a small satisfied nod, an
unhurried click. The desk is tidy: no paper stacks, just a coffee and the computer.

The monitor faces the camera at a slight angle, but the screen shows only a soft out-of-focus
glow of deep NAVY BLUE with warm GOLD accents and abstract card shapes — no legible text, no
readable charts, just the impression of a clean premium dashboard. Warm golden hour light,
inviting modern color grade.

Camera: slow gentle push-in, then settle.
Mood: calm mastery, effortless control, quiet confidence.
Audio: soft calm ambience, a single satisfied exhale, a gentle UI chime.
Style: photorealistic, cinematic 35mm, warm premium lighting, natural film grain.
```

> **Post note:** Shot 3 is where you corner-pin the REAL FinRep dashboard onto the monitor (or
> drop it into a floating navy UI card beside the person). The navy+gold out-of-focus glow makes
> the composite seam invisible. Add the Penny coin in the corner of the screen with a blink.

## Notes specific to this concept

- The **particle-dissolve consolidation (Shot 2)** is the hero idea — spend your best generation
  budget there. It's also the hardest for Veo; generate 3–4 takes and pick the cleanest collapse.
- Crowd scenes can produce warped faces in the background — that's fine here since Shot 1 is a
  wide/high angle and they're meant to blur and dissolve anyway. Keep the crowd distant.
- Keep the **cold-blue → warm-gold** transition consistent with Concept 1 so both films feel like
  one brand campaign.
